--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CHANGE 1 — one leg per player (and per team) in any single multi
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
In afl_bot/build/report.py search_match_sgms, where candidate combos are formed
(the itertools.combinations over the leg pool), REJECT any combo in which two
legs share the same subject. Each LegCandidate already carries `subject` (the
player name for props, the team name for h2h/totals — see cli.py: player legs
are LegCandidate(..., player_name, ...), h2h legs LegCandidate(..., team, ...)).

Rule: keep a combo only if
        len({leg.subject for leg in combo}) == len(combo)
i.e. all subjects distinct. This gives one leg per player automatically, and as
a bonus stops two team/total legs on the same side. Apply it to the SHARED combo
generation so both the model ladder and the Sportsbet ladder inherit it.

Make sure the de-dup happens BEFORE rung selection so each band still gets the
best DISTINCT-player combo (don't just drop the rung). The "every match gets a
full ladder" guarantee stays.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
CHANGE 2 — more multis per game (expand the ladder)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Today MULTI_TARGET_ODDS = (2.10, 3.00, 5.00) — only 3 rungs. Widen it to give a
fuller spread of options from safe to longshot, e.g. in config.py:

    MULTI_TARGET_ODDS = (2.10, 2.75, 3.50, 5.00, 8.00, 15.00)   # 6 rungs

Both ladders should produce one rung per band as they do now (land at-or-longer
than each target, never short; top band = the VALUE PICK by best positive edge).
Keep one-combo-per-rung de-dup so the same combo can't fill two bands. If a band
can't be reached with distinct-player legs, fall back to the closest remaining
combo and tag it model-only as today.

(These targets are config — Ben can widen/narrow later. If 6 rungs makes any
single game's pool too thin to fill every band with distinct players, fill what
you can and note the skipped band rather than repeating a combo.)
