WHAT BEN IS REACTING TO (two linked problems in reports/2026_r16_report.md)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PROBLEM A — the ladder offers legs you cannot actually place.
  e.g. "Will Ashcroft 15+ disposals" (91%), "Callum Mills 15+ disposals" (87%),
  "Lachie Whitfield 15+ disposals" (95%). Books don't post a 15+ disposals line
  on a genuine gun mid — it's a near-lock, not a market. These are phantom legs.

PROBLEM B — the multi price drifts too short (~$1.50).
  GWS rung shows Band $1.75 but Fair odds **$1.59** (63%) — BELOW the band.
  Two causes stacking:
    (1) SGM_LADDER_LEG_PROB_MAX=0.95 lets near-lock legs (the same phantoms from
        Problem A) into the multi pool, so the joint product collapses toward 1.
    (2) The "never land shorter than the band" rule in search_match_sgms
        (_select_for_target) is enforced on the PRE-calibration joint. Then
        apply_multi_calibration() inflates the joint (e.g. 57% -> 63%) and
        recomputes fair_odds DOWN to $1.59 — after the guard has already passed.
        So the displayed price can sit below the band even though selection
        "landed at/above" it.

ROOT INSIGHT (why the last fix was wrong): FIX-LADDER-TARGET-ODDS added near-lock
legs ON PURPOSE so a real ~$1.75 3-leg multi could exist (0.78^3 = $2.11 floor
otherwise). But those near-lock legs ARE the unplaceable phantoms. You can't have
both. Ben's decision: drop the phantoms, accept the honest floor.
